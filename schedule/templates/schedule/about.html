About Revolt Gallery

Since our opening in 2017, Taos Revolt Gallery has sought to offer a space for local, emerging, and underground artists to be heard in the TAOS community. We offer a reflection of New Mexico's unique and evolving cultural landscape through Taos' growing local talent, alongside a number of international artists who share our vision. As of 2020, Taos Revolt Gallery renewed its mission to support artists from diverse backgrounds and elevate the voices of Black, Indigenous, People of Color, and LGBTQIA+ people. We seek to build equality through a strong, inclusive and creative community. Taos Revolt Gallery is committed to artistic and creative freedom for all.

Co-Curator

Sarah Stolar

Sarah Stolar

Sarah Stolar is an interdisciplinary artist living in Santa Fe, New Mexico. The breadth of her work includes painting, drawing, multimedia installation, film, video, and performance art. She holds a BFA in Painting from the Art Academy of Cincinnati (2000), and an MFA in New Genres from the San Francisco Art Institute (2003) and is exhibited in solo exhibitions at the New Mexico Museum of Art, Harwood Museum of Art, and BGMoCA in Montevideo, Uruguay. Sarah, an internationally award-winning filmmaker, including Best Experimental Film at Synergy Film Festival, New York City and finalist for the Andy Warhol Award for Best Experimental Film in the US. A committed educator for over fifteen years, Sarah Stolar is currently the Chair of the Department of Fine Arts, Film and Digital Media at the University of New Mexico - Taos.

Co-Curator

Laurel Taylor

Laurel Taylor

Laurel Taylor, aka Wilde.Ink, is a multidisciplinary artist based in Taos, New Mexico. At her print studio in Arroyo Seco, NM, she hand carves imagery inspired by the surrounding nature, wildlife, and the expanses of the mind and spirit. With Wilde.Ink imagery, Laurel creates apparel and accessories adorned with hand-pulled prints, large-scale hand-dyed and handmade cotton tapestries in meditative patterns, and works on paper.

Formerly the location of the Hulse-Warman Gallery, Taos Revolt has transformed its historic downtown site into a dynamic, thriving facility for artistic exploration. With unique indoor and outdoor areas, we enjoy hosting a variety of events and exhibits - from small to large scale installations, immersive music, art, performances, and more. Taos Revolt is thrilled to present an ever-changing rotation of exhibits. Come view the art at the gallery, listen to live music, enjoy local food, and be with community.
